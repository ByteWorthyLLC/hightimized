How it works

Three steps. The whole thing runs on your phone or laptop. The hospital never knows you ran it.

01

Upload your EOB or itemized bill

PDF, photo, or screenshot. The Explanation of Benefits from your insurer or the itemized statement from the hospital both work.

02

hightimized reads the codes

It explains every line in plain English. CPT codes, revenue codes, modifiers, and the chargemaster price the hospital is required to publish.

03

Generates a dispute letter

If something looks wrong, you get a certified-mail-ready dispute letter that cites the exact regulation the hospital broke. Print, sign, mail.

What it catches

The five most common ways hospitals overbill. hightimized flags every line that looks off and tells you why.

Duplicate billing

The same procedure billed twice on the same day, often under a slightly different code. One of the most common errors and the easiest to dispute.

Upcoding

You were billed for a more expensive procedure than the one actually performed. A 15-minute consult coded as a 45-minute consult. A simple suture coded as a complex repair.

Unbundling

One procedure split across multiple line items so the hospital can charge for each piece. Bundled CPT codes are cheaper. Hospitals know this.

Services not rendered

Lines for tests, supplies, or visits that did not happen. Common with phantom physician fees and consults you do not remember having.

Incorrect patient info

A wrong birth date, member ID, or subscriber name can void coverage and push the full charge onto you. Catch it before it sticks.

Chargemaster overage

Federal law requires hospitals to publish a chargemaster. hightimized cross-checks every line against it and flags charges that exceed the listed price.
